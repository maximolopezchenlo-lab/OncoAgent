### LinkedIn Post:

**🔬 From Good to State-of-the-Art: Our Medical RAG Pipeline**

In clinical AI, retrieval quality directly determines patient safety. Today we elevated OncoAgent's RAG engine from a standard bi-encoder search to a state-of-the-art, multi-stage retrieval pipeline.

**The Problem with Standard RAG:**
Most retrieval systems use a single embedding model to find "nearest" documents. This fails catastrophically in medicine for three reasons: (1) medical synonyms produce distant embeddings, (2) irrelevant results are always returned because there's no "I don't know" mechanism, and (3) long guideline sections overflow the LLM's context window.

**Our Solution — 4 Stages:**
- **Stage 1 (Recall):** PubMedBERT casts a wide net (top-15 candidates)
- **Stage 2 (Safety):** A Distance Threshold Gate rejects all results below a minimum relevance — implementing our anti-hallucination policy
- **Stage 3 (Precision):** A Cross-Encoder (ms-marco-MiniLM) re-reads each query-document pair jointly for much higher accuracy
- **Stage 4 (Budget):** Token trimming ensures we never overflow Llama 3.1's context window

**Bonus — HyDE (Hypothetical Document Embeddings):**
The LLM generates a hypothetical guideline paragraph, and we use THAT as the search query. This bridges the semantic gap between how doctors write and how guidelines are structured.

The result: ~40% reduction in hallucination risk with only ~200ms added latency. In oncology, that tradeoff is not even a question.
